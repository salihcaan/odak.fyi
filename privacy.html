1. Who I am

Hi, I'm Hasan. I built Odak and I run it solo from Istanbul. When this page says "I", that's me — when it says "Odak", that's the app and the business.

Email me at hello@odak.fyi.

Checkout is handled by Paddle, my Merchant of Record. Their privacy policy: paddle.com/legal/privacy.

2. The website

odak.fyi does not track you. I don't care who you are or how you got here.

It does not contain:

  • Analytics (no Google Analytics, Plausible, Fathom, PostHog)
  • Tracking cookies, ad pixels, or remarketing
  • Session replay or heatmaps

It stores one thing in your browser: odak-theme in localStorage, so the site remembers light or dark. It never leaves your browser.

Two requests do leave your browser, neither of them mine: web fonts (Inter and JetBrains Mono) load from Google Fonts, and the server hosting odak.fyi writes a normal HTTP access log (IP, user-agent, path, timestamp) which I keep for up to 30 days for abuse prevention and never use for profiling.

3. The macOS app

Odak runs locally on your Mac, signed with the hardened runtime and Apple-notarized. Two rules:

Nothing that identifies you or your work is ever transmitted. No project names or paths, no file contents, no search queries, no window titles, no email, no account, no IP.

Anonymous usage events and crash reports are off by default. Opt in once during onboarding (or any time in Settings → Privacy) and Odak will share the events listed below — and nothing else. Flipping the toggle off stops both events and crashes immediately.

Stays on your device

None of the following ever leaves your Mac. It lives in macOS application support directories and syncs nowhere:

  • Project list, paths, branch names, and which projects you've opened
  • Search queries you type into the search panel
  • Frecency rankings, search history, and per-project variables in .odak files
  • Window titles read via the macOS Accessibility permission (used only by the IDE switcher)
  • All settings and preferences

Anonymous usage analytics — only if you opt in

Odak uses PostHog as a data processor, configured for the strictest privacy mode it offers:

  • No person profiles (personProfiles = .never) — events carry no user record
  • No identify() — your email is never sent to PostHog, not even after activating a license
  • No automatic capture — no lifecycle, no screen views, no session replay; only the explicit events below
  • No IP collection

The complete list of events Odak can ever send, and the data attached:

  • search_opened, actions_panel_opened — no properties
  • filter_applied — the kind of filter (e.g. folder, ide, uncommitted, tech_stack_swift)
  • project_opened — IDE name, query length as a number, and detected tech stacks (e.g. swift, rust)
  • action_executed — action kind (open_in_app, open_url, run_script) and, for scripts, the language and output mode. Custom action names are never sent.
  • action_imported — count of imported actions
  • window_switched — IDE name only (e.g. cursor, vscode). Window titles are never sent.
  • remote_repo_opened — git host bucketed into one of github_com, gitlab_com, bitbucket_org, azure_devops, codeberg, sourcehut, self_hosted, or unknown. Self-hosted hostnames are never sent.
  • License events (license_activated, license_deactivated, license_activation_failed, trial_started) — carry only non-identifying counters and coded reasons. Never your email, name, or license code.

PostHog may log standard request metadata (user-agent) under its own privacy policy. The PostHog project has IP collection disabled.

Anonymous crash reports — only if you opt in

With telemetry on, Odak uses PostHog's error-tracking module to capture uncaught exceptions, Mach exceptions, and POSIX signals. Reports are written to disk and sent as anonymous $exception events on the next launch. Each one contains a symbolicated stack trace, the app version, and the macOS version — and nothing that ties the crash back to you (no user code, no project content, no paths outside the Odak bundle). The same Settings → Privacy toggle controls events and crashes together.

Update checks (Sparkle)

Odak uses the open-source Sparkle framework to check for updates. Roughly once a day while the app is running, it fetches odak.fyi/appcast.xml. That hits the same access log described in section 2. Sparkle sends nothing about you, your projects, or your usage — just the HTTP request needed to download the file. Update binaries come from GitHub Releases, which logs the download under GitHub's own policy.

4. Purchases & Paddle

When you buy Odak, checkout is run by Paddle, my Merchant of Record. Paddle collects what payment and tax law require:

  • Name and email
  • Billing address and country (for tax)
  • Payment method details (handled by Paddle's PCI-compliant systems — I never see your card number)
  • IP address (for fraud prevention)

Paddle is the controller of your payment data under its own privacy policy. After a successful purchase, Paddle shares a limited record with me — your name, email, country, and what you bought — so I can deliver your license key, answer support, and send essential transactional emails (license delivery, receipts, important update notices). I never receive your card details.

5. Support email

If you write to support@odak.fyi or hello@odak.fyi, your email sits in my mailbox until I reply. I don't add anyone to a marketing list — there isn't one.

6. Your rights

Depending on where you live, you may have rights under the EU/UK GDPR, the California Consumer Privacy Act (CCPA/CPRA), Turkey's KVKK, or similar laws. These usually include the right to:

  • Access the personal data I hold about you
  • Correct inaccurate data
  • Delete your data ("right to be forgotten")
  • Object to or restrict certain processing
  • Receive your data in a portable format
  • Withdraw consent where processing is based on consent
  • Lodge a complaint with your local data-protection authority

To exercise any of these for data I hold, email hello@odak.fyi. For payment data held by Paddle, contact Paddle at paddle.net.

7. Retention

  • Server access logs: up to 30 days.
  • Purchase records (name, email, transaction ID, license): kept as long as tax and accounting law require (typically 5–10 years), then deleted.
  • Support email: kept until it's no longer useful, generally up to 24 months after the last reply.

8. Changes

I'll update this page when the product or my processors change. The "Last updated" date at the top reflects the latest revision. Material changes that affect paying customers go out by email.
